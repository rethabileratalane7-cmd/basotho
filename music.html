Rhythms of the Basotho people

Music and dance are central to Basotho celebrations,ritual, and storytelling. Traditional instruments like the Lesiba and Setolo-tolo create unique sounds that accompany dances performed during festivals and ceremonies.

TRADITIONAL DANCES & INSTRUMENTS

DANCES

    mokhibo

    Is a traitional dance performed by Basotho women,especially during cultural celebrations and important gatherings.

    mokorotlo

    Male war dance and chant

    Mohobelo

    Male stomp dance with powerful movements.

    Liphotha

    Initiation dance (boys/men)

    Liba

    Performed during traditional ceremonies

    Litolobonya

    Women's celebratory dance

    Lefufa

    Aggressive war-style dance

    Motholo-tholo

    Playful children dance

    Liphotha tsa banana

    Girls initiation dance

    Mokhele

    Rhythmic communal dance

    Motjeko

    General word for dancing (also used as a type of dance)

    Litolobonya tsa Lenyalo

    Wedding dances

INSTRUMENTS

    Lekope

    Traditional bow string instruments

    Setolo-tolo

    Mouth-resonated instrument played by humming

    Thomo

    Plicked mouth harp

    Mokhoro

    Calabash drum

    Lefu

    Animal horn used for sound

    Phala

    Reed flute

    Moropa

    Drum

    Seholi

    Rattle made of seed pods or shells

    Fere

    whistle

    Lesiba

    Stringed wind instrument played using mouth
